SECTION 1 — PRODUCT OVERVIEW

1. Product Overview
1.1 Problem Statement
Real estate agents in Nigeria today run their entire business through informal channels — primarily WhatsApp status updates and broadcast lists. This creates four critical problems:

	•	Limited reach — only people who already have the agent's phone number can see their listings. New clients are acquired almost entirely through word of mouth, which is unreliable and slow.
	•	No professional credibility signal — there is no verifiable record of an agent's listings, track record, or legitimacy. Renters have no way to distinguish trustworthy agents from fraudsters.
	•	No structured deal flow — enquiries come through scattered DMs with no tracking, no history, and no way to manage multiple interested parties at once.
	•	Inventory disappears — listings posted as WhatsApp statuses expire after 24 hours. There is no searchable, persistent record of what an agent has available.

DenHunt Agent solves all four problems by giving every real estate agent in Nigeria a professional platform to list, manage, and grow their property business.

1.2 Product Vision
DenHunt Agent is the professional home for every Nigerian real estate agent — from the solo operator posting flats on their phone to the mid-size agency managing a team of 10. It replaces the WhatsApp-based workflow with a structured, verifiable, and scalable platform that makes agents more credible, more reachable, and more effective at closing deals.

1.3 Core Loop
The single most important flow in Version 1 is:

This loop must work flawlessly before anything else is built. Every screen, every API call, and every design decision in Version 1 serves this loop.

SECTION 3 — ONBOARDING FLOWS

3. Onboarding Flows
3.1 App Entry & Role Selection
When a user opens DenHunt Agent for the first time, they land on a splash screen followed by a role selection screen. This is the first decision gate.

Screen: Role Selection
	•	Headline: "What brings you to DenHunt?"
	•	Option 1: "I'm an individual agent" — solo agents posting their own listings
	•	Option 2: "I represent a real estate agency" — agency owner/manager setting up a team account
	•	Small text below: "Looking to rent a place? Download DenHunt Home" (links to renter app store page — Phase 2)
	•	No login/signup on this screen — role selection comes first

3.2 Individual Agent Onboarding
6-step linear flow. Must be completed before the agent can post listings.

Step 1 — Phone Number
	•	Input: Nigerian phone number (auto-format, +234 prefix)
	•	Action: Send OTP via SMS (Africa's Talking or Twilio)
	•	Validation: must be valid Nigerian mobile number
	•	Error states: invalid number, OTP failed, OTP expired (resend after 60s)

Step 2 — OTP Verification
	•	6-digit OTP input field, auto-advance on last digit
	•	60-second countdown timer with resend option
	•	On success: Supabase Auth creates user session with role = individual_agent

Step 3 — Basic Profile
	•	Full name (required)
	•	Email address (optional at this stage)
	•	Profile photo — camera or gallery, cropped to square, 1:1 ratio
	•	This data writes to the users table immediately

Step 4 — Professional Details
	•	Years of experience (dropdown: Less than 1 year, 1–3 years, 3–5 years, 5–10 years, 10+ years)
	•	Primary areas of operation — multi-select from a predefined list of Nigerian states and LGAs, with search
	•	Property types they specialise in — multi-select: Apartments, Duplexes, Self-contain, Commercial, Land, Short-let

Step 5 — Identity Verification
	•	Government ID type selection: NIN, Driver's Licence, International Passport, Voter's Card
	•	Upload front of ID — camera or gallery
	•	Upload back of ID (where applicable — not for passport)
	•	BVN entry field — 11 digits, masked after entry
Note: BVN is verified asynchronously via Youverify or Dojah API. The agent can proceed to Step 6 while verification runs in the background. Verification status shows as "Pending" on their profile until confirmed.

Step 6 — Agreement & Completion
	•	Display DenHunt Terms of Service and Agent Code of Conduct (scrollable, must scroll to bottom to enable button)
	•	Checkbox: "I agree to the Terms of Service and Agent Code of Conduct"
	•	On confirm: agent lands on the home dashboard with a welcome state
	•	A "Verification Pending" banner shows at the top of the dashboard until BVN verification completes

3.3 Real Estate Agency Onboarding
The agency admin goes through a similar flow with additional agency-specific steps.

Steps 1–3 — Same as Individual Agent
Phone, OTP, and basic profile steps are identical. Role is set to agency_admin.

Step 4 — Agency Details
	•	Agency name (required)
	•	CAC registration number (required — format: RC followed by 6–7 digits)
	•	Agency logo — camera or gallery upload, cropped to square
	•	Office address
	•	Agency website (optional)
	•	Number of agents currently in the team (optional — for internal analytics)

Step 5 — Document Verification
	•	Upload CAC certificate (PDF or image)
	•	Admin's government ID (same options as individual agent)
	•	Admin's BVN
Note: Agency verification is reviewed manually by a DenHunt admin before the agency account is marked active. The admin can invite agents and set up the account while verification is pending, but listings go live only after verification is approved.

Step 6 — Agreement & Completion
Same as individual agent. On completion, agency admin lands on the agency dashboard.

3.4 Agency Agent Onboarding (Invite Flow)
Agency agents do not self-register. They join via an invite link.

	•	Agency admin goes to Team section and taps "Invite Agent"
	•	System generates a unique invite link with a 7-day expiry
	•	Admin shares the link (copy/WhatsApp share sheet)
	•	Agent taps the link, opens the app (or app store if not installed)
	•	Agent sees: "You've been invited to join [Agency Name] on DenHunt"
	•	Agent completes: phone, OTP, full name, profile photo, government ID
	•	No BVN required for agency agents — the agency's verification covers them
	•	On completion, role = agency_agent, linked to the agency via agency_members table

SECTION 4 — LISTING MANAGEMENT

4. Listing Management
4.1 Listing Creation Flow
Creating a listing is the core action of the agent app. The flow must be fast, intuitive, and forgiving — agents should be able to create a complete listing from their phone in under 5 minutes.

Step 1 — Property Category
	•	What are you listing? (required, single select):
	•	Apartment / Flat
	•	Self-contained
	•	Mini flat
	•	Duplex / Terrace
	•	Bungalow
	•	Mansion / Detached
	•	Room & Parlour (Room & Sitting Room)
	•	Single room
	•	Commercial space (office, shop, warehouse)
	•	Land
	•	Short-let
	•	Listing purpose (required, single select): For Rent / For Sale / Short-let

Step 2 — Media Upload
Media is the most important part of a listing. Agents should upload at minimum 3 photos before a listing can be submitted.
	•	Photos: camera capture + gallery upload. Minimum 3, maximum 20.
	•	Video: optional. Maximum 1 video, maximum 60 seconds, maximum 100MB.
	•	Photos are uploaded to Supabase Storage. Each image is compressed client-side to max 1.5MB before upload using expo-image-manipulator.
	•	Agent can reorder photos by drag-and-drop. First photo becomes the cover image.
	•	Each photo shows a delete icon. If below minimum (3), a warning prevents proceeding.
	•	Video is uploaded separately and stored with a reference on the listing record.

4.3 Listing Management Dashboard
The agent's home screen after login. Shows:

	•	Sort options: Newest, Most enquiries, Most views
	•	Filter by: status, property type, area
	•	Floating action button: + New Listing

4.4 Listing Edit Rules
	•	A pending_review listing cannot be edited. Agent must wait for admin decision.
	•	An active listing can be edited. On save, status returns to pending_review for re-review.
	•	A draft can be freely edited at any time.
	•	Price changes on an active listing trigger a re-review.
	•	Photo-only updates on an active listing do not require re-review (they are reviewed separately by admin in background).

SECTION 5 — SUBSCRIPTION & PAYWALL

5. Subscription & Paywall
5.1 Paywall Strategy
The paywall is introduced after the agent has verified their identity and created their first listing. This is intentional — we want agents to experience the value of the platform before asking them to pay. A cold paywall on signup kills conversion.

The trigger: when an agent tries to submit their first listing for review, the subscription screen appears. They must select a plan and complete payment before the listing is submitted.

Once subscribed, subsequent listings are submitted directly. The paywall only appears at first listing submission, and at renewal time.

	•	Use Paystack's React Native SDK for in-app payment

	•	On expiry: listings auto-paused (not deleted). Agent prompted to renew on next app open.

5.5 Free Trial
For launch only: 14-day free trial on the Pro plan for all new signups. After 14 days, the paywall appears. No credit card required to start the trial. This is the mechanism to acquire the first 500 agents.

SECTION 6 — ENQUIRY MANAGEMENT

6. Enquiry Management
6.1 How Enquiries Work
In Phase 1, a renter cannot use DenHunt directly (renter app is Phase 2). However, enquiries are still important in Phase 1 because:
	•	Listings are publicly accessible via a web link that agents can share (a web listing view — not a full web app, just a shareable listing page)
	•	Agents can share their DenHunt listing link on WhatsApp, Instagram, anywhere — and interested renters can submit an enquiry form from that web page
	•	This gives agents a structured, trackable replacement for WhatsApp DMs from day one

6.2 Enquiry Data
When a renter submits an enquiry from a shared listing link, they provide:
	•	Full name (required)
	•	Phone number (required)
	•	Email (optional)
	•	Message (optional — pre-filled with "I'm interested in this property. Please contact me.")
	•	Preferred inspection date (optional — date picker)

6.3 Enquiry Inbox
The agent sees all enquiries in an Inbox section of the app:
	•	Each enquiry card shows: enquirer name, phone, listing title, time received, status
	•	Status options: New, Contacted, Inspection Scheduled, Deal Closed, Not Interested
	•	Agent taps an enquiry to see full details and update status
	•	Quick actions: Schedule inspection, Mark as Contacted, Call (secondary). NO WhatsApp/SMS/email links — see §6.4. Chat opens only after the renter pays the inspection fee (escrow), see §6.5.
	•	Filter by: listing, status, date
	•	New enquiries trigger a push notification

6.4 In-App Communication & Anti-Fraud Policy (DIRECTIVE)
All renter↔agent communication MUST happen inside DenHunt. This is a core anti-fraud measure: conversations and transactions that move to WhatsApp/SMS/email cannot be moderated or monitored, scams proliferate, and DenHunt's trust guarantee collapses. Keeping comms on-platform is also the foundation of trust infrastructure (moderation, reporting, real-time intervention, conversion analytics).
- No external communication deep links (WhatsApp/SMS/mailto) as actions anywhere. The in-app chat is the channel. A phone "Call" affordance may remain as a secondary action only.
- Every message is stored on-platform for moderation, dispute review, and escrow evidence (§14). Users can report a message or a conversation.
- If a user goes off-platform and is defrauded, DenHunt is not responsible — but the product must never push them off-platform.

6.5 Chat is escrow-gated and lives inside an inspection session
Chat is NOT standalone. The flow:
1. Enquiry — renter submits the form (name + interest only; phone NOT shared yet).
2. Agent schedules an inspection (date). Renter gets push + email.
3. Renter pays the inspection fee → held in escrow. This unlocks the chat for both parties. Until then only names are shown — no phone/personal details.
4. Chat opens. Real-time, WhatsApp-grade: text + image + system messages, optimistic send, read receipts, typing indicator, keyboard-safe input, swipe-to-reply (Phase 1).
5. Reschedule is a dynamic in-chat negotiation: either side proposes a new date; the other accepts or counter-proposes. Confirmed only on mutual agreement.
6. Inspection happens (6-digit code, §14) → 8-hour escrow countdown → released to agent, or refunded on dispute.

Data model (escrow-gated chat):
- inspection_sessions — conversation spine: id, enquiry_id, listing_id, agent_id, renter_id (nullable Phase 1), renter_name, inspection_fee, inspection_code, scheduled_date, status (scheduled | reschedule_pending | escrow_held | in_progress | completed | disputed | refunded | cancelled), proposed_date, proposed_by (agent|renter), chat_unlocked, code_confirmed_at, escrow_release_at, last_message, last_message_at, paystack_reference, created_at, updated_at
- messages — id, session_id, sender_role (agent|renter|system), sender_id, type (text|image|system), body, image_url, reply_to (→ messages.id), created_at, read_at
- Real-time via Supabase Realtime; typing via Realtime presence; optimistic send on the client.
- Renter side: Phase 1 web listing page (Phase 2 renter app). Agent side built now; seed/mock data drives all phases.

SECTION 8 — ADMIN MODERATION PANEL

8. Admin Moderation Panel
8.1 Overview
The admin panel is a web-based dashboard (not a mobile app) used by DenHunt staff to review listings, verify agents, manage reports, and monitor platform health. It is accessible only to users with role = admin, which is set manually in the database.

8.2 Listing Review
	•	Queue of all pending_review listings sorted by submission time (oldest first)
	•	Each listing shows: all photos, video, all details, agent info, BVN verification status
	•	Admin actions: Approve (sets status to active), Reject (with mandatory reason text)
	•	Rejection reasons: Misleading information, Duplicate listing, Poor photo quality, Incomplete details, Fraudulent listing, Other
	•	On approval: agent notified via push notification — "Your listing is now live"
	•	On rejection: agent notified with the rejection reason — "Your listing was rejected: [reason]. Please edit and resubmit."
	•	Admin can flag an agent for review from within the listing review screen

8.3 Agent Verification
	•	Queue of pending agent verifications
	•	Shows: uploaded ID documents, BVN verification result from KYC API, CAC document (for agencies)
	•	Actions: Approve verification, Reject with reason, Request additional documents
	•	Verified agents get a verification badge on their profile and listings

8.4 Agent Management
	•	Search and view all agents
	•	View agent's full profile, listings, and enquiry history
	•	Actions: Suspend agent (all listings paused), Ban agent (account deactivated, listings removed), Unsuspend
	•	Suspension reason is recorded and agent is notified

8.5 Platform Metrics Dashboard
	•	Total registered agents (by type: individual, agency admin, agency agent)
	•	Total active listings
	•	New signups today / this week / this month
	•	Listings submitted for review / approved / rejected (daily)
	•	Subscription revenue (by plan, by period)
	•	Enquiries submitted through listing pages

SECTION 14 — INSPECTION ESCROW & DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14. Inspection Escrow & Dispute Resolution
14.1 Why This Exists
Nigeria’s rental market has a severe trust problem. Agents post videos and photos of properties that do not match reality. Renters pay inspection fees and arrive to find a completely different property. There is no recourse, no accountability, and no intermediary. DenHunt’s inspection escrow system is the single feature that most differentiates the platform from every other listing site in Nigeria. It protects renters from fraud, protects legitimate agents from false accusations, and gives DenHunt the role of neutral trusted arbitrator in every transaction.
14.2 Inspection Fee Model
Each agent sets their own inspection fee on a per-listing basis. Multiple agents can list the same property at different inspection fees. The renter chooses which agent to book an inspection with. DenHunt does not take a cut of the inspection fee. The full amount is held in escrow and disbursed to the agent after a successful inspection.
	•	Inspection fee is set by the agent at listing creation or edit time. It is a required field before a listing can go live.
	•	The renter pays the inspection fee through the DenHunt platform (Paystack) before an inspection is confirmed. No off-platform payments are permitted.
	•	Funds are held by DenHunt in escrow. DenHunt must hold these via a licensed escrow partner (MFB or regulated fintech) — not in a generic business account.
	•	Funds are released to the agent 8 hours after inspection begins (code shared), provided no dispute has been raised. Silence within 8 hours is treated as a successful inspection.
	•	If a dispute is raised, the escrow hold is extended indefinitely until admin resolves it.
14.3 Inspection Booking Flow
	•	Renter views a listing and taps “Book Inspection”
	•	Renter selects preferred date and time for inspection
	•	Renter pays the agent’s inspection fee via Paystack (card, bank transfer, USSD). Payment is held in escrow immediately.
	•	System generates a unique 6-digit inspection code and sends it to the renter only. Agent does not see the code.
	•	Agent receives notification: “Inspection booked for [Property Title] on [Date] at [Time]. A renter will present a code on arrival.”
	•	On the day: renter arrives at the property. Before entering, renter shares their 6-digit code with the agent verbally or by showing their screen.
	•	Agent enters the code into the DenHunt Agent app. System validates the code and marks the inspection as “In Progress”. The 8-hour escrow countdown begins.
	•	Both parties receive a notification: “Inspection has begun. Payment will be released to the agent in 8 hours unless a dispute is raised.”
	•	After 8 hours with no dispute: escrow releases to agent. Both parties notified.
14.4 Evidence Recording During Inspection
Once inspection begins (code confirmed), both parties are encouraged to record evidence. This protects both sides in the event of a dispute.
Agent Evidence (Agent App)
	•	During an active inspection session, the agent sees a prominent “Record Inspection” button in the app.
	•	The agent can record video of the renter physically inside the property. This establishes that the renter visited the correct building.
	•	Video is uploaded directly through the app and stored against the inspection session record. It is timestamped and cannot be edited or deleted by the agent after upload.
	•	This recording is only surfaced to DenHunt admin in the event of a dispute. It is not visible to the renter.
Renter Evidence (Renter App)
	•	If the renter believes the property does not match the listing, they can upload photo and video evidence directly through the renter app during the active inspection window.
	•	Evidence must be submitted from within the app using the live camera only. Gallery uploads are not accepted as dispute evidence, to prevent submission of pre-recorded or unrelated footage.
	•	All dispute media is timestamped server-side on upload and stored against the inspection session ID.
14.5 Dispute Resolution Flow
A dispute can be raised by the renter at any point during the active inspection window (from code confirmation to 8 hours after).
Step 1 — Renter Raises Dispute
	•	Renter taps “Raise Dispute” in the renter app during the active inspection window.
	•	Renter selects dispute reason: Property does not match listing photos/video | Property description is inaccurate | Wrong property shown | Safety concern | Other
	•	Renter submits live-camera photo and/or video evidence (gallery upload not accepted).
	•	System captures renter’s GPS coordinates at the moment of dispute submission.
Step 2 — GPS Proximity Check (Automated)
	•	System compares renter’s GPS at dispute time against the listing’s registered coordinates.
	•	System also requests and captures the agent’s GPS at the same time via a notification prompt to the agent app.
	•	GPS threshold: if both renter and agent are within 150 metres of the listing’s coordinates, the system flags that the inspection physically occurred at the correct location. This is passed to the admin as a supporting signal, not a final verdict.
	•	Admin can override the GPS signal in cases of known drift (dense urban areas, GPS inaccuracy indoors).
Step 3 — Admin Review
The admin dispute review screen shows all evidence side by side:
	•	Original listing photos and video (what was advertised)
	•	Renter’s dispute photos/video (what the renter says they found)
	•	Agent’s inspection recording (agent’s counter-evidence showing renter inside the property)
	•	GPS proximity result and map view
	•	Renter’s dispute reason and written description
Step 4 — Admin Verdict & Consequences
Admin selects one of three verdicts:
	•	Dispute upheld (agent at fault): Escrow refunded to renter. Listing taken down immediately pending full review. Agent receives a strike. Strike consequences: 1st strike = warning + mandatory listing re-review, 2nd strike = 30-day suspension, 3rd strike = permanent ban.
	•	Dispute rejected (renter at fault / fraudulent dispute): Escrow released to agent. Renter receives a warning. Repeat fraudulent disputes result in renter account suspension. This protects legitimate agents from bad-faith claims.
	•	Inconclusive: Admin requests additional evidence from either party. Escrow remains held. 48-hour window for additional evidence submission before admin makes a final call.

SECTION 15 — PERSONAL INSPECTOR

15. Personal Inspector
15.1 What Is a Personal Inspector
A Personal Inspector is a DenHunt staff role. These are vetted, hyperlocal individuals recruited by DenHunt who live close to the areas they cover. When a renter cannot attend an inspection themselves (due to distance, schedule, or preference), they can request a Personal Inspector to visit the property on their behalf and send back a full structured report with photos and video.
Personal Inspectors are not agents. They do not post listings. They do not represent landlords. They are neutral third parties working exclusively for DenHunt and paid per completed report.
15.2 Personal Inspector Role in the Database
	•	Role value added to users table: personal_inspector
	•	Accounts are created by DenHunt admin only. Personal inspectors cannot self-register.
	•	Each inspector profile includes: name, phone, coverage areas (array of LGAs), active status, total reports completed, average rating.
15.3 Pricing Model
	•	Renter pays ₦2,500 to DenHunt per personal inspection request.
	•	DenHunt pays the assigned inspector ₦800–₦1,500 per completed and approved report (rate varies by area complexity and report quality, set by DenHunt internally).
	•	DenHunt gross margin per report: ₦1,000–₦1,700.
	•	Inspector payment is released after the report is submitted and passes admin quality review.
15.4 Personal Inspection Request Flow
	•	Renter views a listing and taps “Request Personal Inspection”
	•	Renter pays ₦2,500 via Paystack. Payment held by DenHunt.
	•	System assigns the request to the nearest available personal inspector covering that LGA. Assignment is automated based on coverage area. If no inspector is available, renter is notified and can request a refund or wait.
	•	Inspector receives push notification with: property address, listing link, agent contact, requested inspection window.
	•	Inspector coordinates with the agent directly (phone or in-app) to schedule the visit.
	•	Inspector visits the property and uses the DenHunt Inspector app to complete the structured report (see 15.5).
	•	Completed report is reviewed by DenHunt admin for quality. On approval, report is sent to the renter and inspector is paid.
15.5 Inspector Report Structure
The inspector app presents a structured checklist. Each section has: a tap-to-select field (Yes/No/Partial or rating), a required photo upload, an optional video clip, and an optional free-text comment. Sections:
	•	Property exterior: building condition, gate/fence, compound, parking
	•	Interior rooms: each room photo, ceiling, walls, flooring, windows, doors
	•	Utilities: water supply tested, power/meter checked, gas
	•	Neighbourhood: road quality, street lighting, proximity to market/transport, noise level, safety observation, flood risk
	•	Listing accuracy check: does the property match the listing photos and description? Inspector rates: Matches accurately | Mostly matches | Significant differences | Does not match
	•	Inspector summary: free text overall assessment, up to 300 characters
	•	Walk-through video: one continuous video walk-through of the entire property, max 3 minutes, mandatory

SECTION 16 — AREA REPORTS & RENTER SEARCH REQUESTS

16. Area Reports & Renter Search Requests
16.1 Area Reports
Area Reports are purchasable intelligence documents about specific neighbourhoods and locations in Nigeria. They are produced by DenHunt’s personal inspectors and reviewed by admin before publication. They give renters and investors comprehensive ground-truth data about an area before committing to a viewing or a lease.
Example: “Gbagada Phase 2, Lagos — Area Report (Updated 7 days ago)”
What an Area Report Covers
	•	Roads & access: road condition, tarred or untarred, flooding risk in rainy season, traffic situation
	•	Safety & security: street lighting, known security concerns, gated vs open streets, police post proximity
	•	Utilities: PHCN reliability, borehole availability, general water supply quality
	•	Amenities & convenience: markets, hospitals, schools, churches/mosques, supermarkets, transport hubs
	•	Typical rent range: self-con, 1-bed, 2-bed, 3-bed approximate price range based on current listings and inspector observation
	•	Community vibe: quiet residential vs commercial bustle, demographic notes, noise levels, overall livability
	•	Photo gallery: minimum 10 photos of the area taken by the inspector during the visit
	•	Date of visit: prominently displayed so buyer knows how recent the data is. Reports older than 90 days are flagged as outdated.
Area Report Pricing & Access
	•	Reports are one-time purchases. Price TBD at launch (suggested: ₦500–₦1,500 per report depending on depth).
	•	A renter can browse report titles and preview the summary section for free. Full report (all sections, photos, video) requires payment.
	•	After purchase, the report is permanently accessible in the renter’s account. No re-purchase required.
	•	Reports are commissioned proactively by DenHunt for high-demand areas, or triggered when a renter requests an area report for a location that does not yet have one (request goes to the inspector queue).
16.2 Renter Search Requests
A Renter Search Request is a paid concierge feature for renters who are urgent, specific, or struggling to find what they need through standard search. The renter fills out a form describing exactly what they need and pays a ₦500 service fee. DenHunt’s team manually searches available listings and matches the best options to the renter within 24–48 hours.
Search Request Form Fields
	•	Property type needed (single select: apartment, self-con, duplex, etc.)
	•	Preferred location(s) — up to 3 areas
	•	Maximum budget per annum (₦)
	•	Number of bedrooms needed
	•	Move-in date (urgency signal)
	•	Must-have amenities (multi-select: 24hr light, borehole, security, parking, etc.)
	•	Additional notes (free text, max 200 characters)
Fulfilment
	•	Renter pays ₦500 via Paystack. Payment is non-refundable (covers search effort regardless of outcome).
	•	Request appears in admin panel. DenHunt team searches active listings and manually curates up to 5 matching options.
	•	Within 24–48 hours, renter receives a notification with their matched listings and a short note from the DenHunt team.
	•	If no suitable listings exist, renter is informed and the search request stays open for 14 days. As new listings come in that match, the renter is notified automatically.
	•	Phase 2 enhancement: renter search requests are also surfaced to verified agents who can proactively match properties to open requests, creating a reverse marketplace dynamic.
